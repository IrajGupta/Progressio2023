POUNDS OF TRASH CLEANED

ABOUT US

Our initiative, Project Olympius, is a water-borne enterprise aiming to create a positive environmental impact, that is based on the UNSDG 14 - Life Below Water. Through the use of robotic, solar-powered boats, we aim to clear the accumulated plastic in the Great Garbage Patch, located in the Pacific Ocean. This plastic is roughly around 79,000 metric tons and makes up 99.9% of marine debris in this area, so to clean up these piles of plastic, we bring in our robot - Oceania - and aim to create a greener world.

OUR GODS OF THE SEAS

THE NEPTUNE

It is a massive boat that contains nets to collect plastics from the depth of the ocean. Since it is large, it has a large storage capacity for collecting plastic which reduces the needfor it to often go back and from the dock.

THE POSEIDON

Since plastic travels along with the water currents, our robot travels across the water current; causing the plastic debris to move in a vortex allowing all the plastic to surround the boat which helps in an easier retrieval process. In order to do so, Poseidon is equipped with high-engine capacity.

THE ACHILLES

It stays at the main sources of plastic pollution (beaches, lakes, etc), which helps prevent the initial stages of pollution and ensures no more plastic gets collected into the Garbage Patch.
