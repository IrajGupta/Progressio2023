Our Mission

PROBLEM

The Great Pacific Garbage Patch is a massive collection of plastic and other debris that has accumulated in the Pacific Ocean. It is estimated to be twice the size of Texas and contains over 1.8 trillion pieces of plastic. The debris can harm marine life, as animals can become entangled in plastic or mistake it for food. This can lead to injury or death. Additionally, the debris can release harmful chemicals into the water, which can harm marine ecosystems. Finally, the patch can contribute to climate change, as the plastic can break down into microplastics that release greenhouse gases when exposed to sunlight. The patch continues to grow, and without action, it could have devastating effects on the environment and the economy. It is important to take steps to reduce plastic waste and prevent it from entering our oceans.

SOLUTION

Our robots possess a trash compactor which increase the amount of plastic that can be collected. Our robots are autonomous and run on solar and hydro energy. This ensures that we have a sustainable source of energy thus reducing costs of maintainance while also have a near zero carbon emmission. Before it collects the plastic, our robot is specially designed to emit high-frequency (sonar) sound waves to deter any fishes or marine animals that could get caught in the nets.
